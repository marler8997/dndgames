# Snapper — Proposal to Make Him a Permanent Party Member

*A pitch for the DM from the Fangs of the Forest (Ruby Phoenix, ~Level 12).*

## The ask, in one breath
> You run Snapper as an instinct-driven protective pet — your call, full stop. We're
> questing toward the **Awaken Animal** ritual; once we pull it off, he becomes a
> free-willed ally who *chooses* to stay, and we take over running him as a party member.

This gives the DM total control now, a **bounded** workload with a clear end date, and a
story beat to build toward. The autonomy shifts hands exactly when the fiction earns it.

---

## Who Snapper is (stat summary)
- **Grave Spinosaurus** — Unique, N, **Gargantuan**, Animal, Dinosaur. **Creature Level 15.**
- **AC** 30 · **HP** 280 · **Fort** +23 · **Ref** +21 · **Will** +19 (mental saves are the soft spot — that's how Dominate eventually landed)
- **Int −4, Wis +3, Cha +3** — a wild beast, not a thinker (this matters for both phases)
- **Speed** 40 ft, swim 30 ft · **Senses** Perception +26, low-light, scent (imprecise) 30 ft
- **Attacks:** jaws +30 (3d12+15 piercing, **reach 20 ft**, deadly d12, Grab); claw +30 (3d8+15 slashing, agile, reach 15 ft)
- **Athletics +30** (+32 Swim) — a monstrous grappler
- **Signature abilities:** Grab · Rip and Tear (6d10 + 2d6 bleed on a grabbed creature) ·
  Swallow Whole (3d12+12) · Staggering Sail (30-ft cone, incapacitation) · Pouncing Pin (Stride + Strike + Trip)

**Honest note:** he is **3 levels above the party**. A permanent level-15 ally is a big
balance lever — which is exactly why the two-phase, DM-controls-it-first structure below
exists. We're handing the DM the leashes up front.

---

## How we got here (for context)
1. Ryu cast **Dominate** on the wild Spinosaurus after it critically failed its save.
2. Ryu later used **Tame Animal** (10 min), letting him issue **Command Animal**.
3. Kei named him **Snapper**. He's fought beside us for weeks; we feed and heal him.

Dominate is the temporary leash (6th level lapses at Ryu's next daily prep). We don't want
to rely on re-casting it forever — hence this plan.

---

## Phase 1 — DM-controlled protective pet (now → Awaken)
While Snapper is Int −4, the DM running him on instinct is both the realistic *and* the
balanced choice. The DM holds the balance dial directly, so he can never be optimized into
a player-driven win button.

### Behavior profile (so it's a fast decision, not a judgment call every turn)
- **Defends Ryu (his tamer) above all** — interposes, attacks whatever threatens him.
- **Goes after the biggest/most obvious threat** to the pack — not the cleverest target.
- **Won't path into hazards or obvious traps** — animal caution.
- **Morale:** if badly hurt, he goes defensive or flees rather than fighting to the death.
- **Responds to tone and gesture, not tactics** — no "flank the caster while we focus the archer."

### The social contract (this is the real unlock)
- **The DM's call on Snapper's actions is final. We won't backseat-drive him.**
- **We'll offload the dice:** a player tracks Snapper's HP and rolls his attacks; the DM
  only dictates *intent*, so a level-15 stat block doesn't clog the DM's turn.
- He's **mortal and exposed** — enemies can target, lure, scare, or kill him. He's a
  resource we protect, not an invincible shield. (If he dies, reviving him costs a real
  resource — e.g., a Phoenix-necklace feather.)

### Optional nudge (a middle ground if the DM wants it)
Snapper stays DM-run, **but** a PC can spend an action on **Command an Animal** (Nature
check) to *request* a specific action, which the DM may honor or interpret. Organic pet
most of the time; an occasional lever when it matters.

## Phase 2 — Awaken Animal ritual (the handoff)
This is the in-world moment that justifies the party taking the reins: Snapper stops being
a wild animal and becomes a thinking ally who stays **by choice**, removing the
mind-control ick entirely.

### Ritual facts
- **Level 3 ritual** · 1 day to cast · **primary check: Nature (master)** · **3 secondary casters**.
- **Crit success:** Int/Wis/Cha each rise to **+2**; Snapper becomes **helpful** to us.
- **Success:** stats rise to **+0**; becomes **friendly**.
- **Failure:** nothing happens (we can try again).
- **Crit failure:** he awakens with *bestial hatred*, turns **hostile**, tries to destroy us. 😬

### Risk read
It's only a **level-3 ritual (DC 20)**. A level-12 caster with master Nature clears it by a
mile — realistically only a natural 1 even fails, and a true *crit* failure is nearly
impossible with a healthy modifier. The scary outcome is mostly flavor, **if** we're
proficient enough.

### Prerequisites checklist
- [ ] **Who has Nature (master)?** Ryu hit Nature *expert* at L7 — confirm someone has
      pushed it to **master**, or line up an NPC (Mei Ling the oracle? the Corsairs?) as primary.
- [ ] **3 secondary casters** trained in the secondary skills (our other three PCs likely cover it).
- [ ] **Aim for the crit success** — helpful + all mental stats at +2 is a smarter, more
      devoted Snapper. Stack bonuses / Aid.
- [ ] **Do it somewhere safe**, and keep him Tamed/Dominated *during* the ritual as
      insurance against the freak bad roll.

### Expectation to set with the DM
"Friendly/helpful" ≠ obedient. Awaken makes Snapper a **free-willed** ally who *likes* us —
his attitude could shift if mistreated. For our arc that's a feature (he's a real character
now). Agree up front on **who runs him post-Awaken** (probably a player, with the DM
voicing personality).

## Tournament legality (Ruby Phoenix)
**There is no rule against a dinosaur on the team.** From the Player's Guide:
- **No maximum team size.** The only disincentive to extra members is that the **prize is
  split** among the whole team.
- **Any weapons or tactics are legal** — no creature-type restriction. Animal companions,
  summons, and beasts are all fair game.
- **Showy, unique tactics please the crowd and judges** (matches our journal note that
  flamboyance earns extra feathers). A dinosaur gladiator is *great* spectacle.
- Our recorded qualifier rules (journal ~898–908: feathers, wagers, challenges, looting,
  disqualification for refusing) say nothing about roster composition either.

### Two questions for the DM
1. **Is Snapper a prize-splitting contender, or Ryu's companion?** If registered as a full
   fighter, he dilutes everyone's prize share *and* becomes a target the enemy can KO to win
   the match. If treated like a companion/pet that comes *with* Ryu (as familiars/companions/
   summons normally do), neither applies.
2. **Arena fit.** Snapper is **Gargantuan (20-ft space, 20-ft reach)**. Open-field Danger
   Island qualifiers fit fine; the **formal Goka arena rounds** use designed battle-maps that
   may be too cramped or hazardous for an oversized combatant — the venue could legitimately
   exclude him there.

**Tie-in:** an *Awakened*, sapient Snapper is far easier to register as a legitimate
contender than a wild animal — another reason Phase 2 is the goal.

## TL;DR for the DM
- **Now:** you run Snapper as an instinctive protective pet (we offload dice, your call is
  final, he can be hurt/scared/killed).
- **Goal:** earn the **Awaken Animal** ritual → he becomes a free-willed ally who chooses to
  stay; we take over running him.
- **Tournament:** rules-legal; just settle "contender vs. companion" and arena fit.
